01 — The pivot

We don't need to own every term.
We own the right ones.

The first pull delivered an important insight before we'd written a single page: the highest-volume keywords are not the ones that matter commercially for CX.

Agiles Projektmanagement (agile project management) has 3,600 monthly searches in Germany; Requirements Engineering has 2,900. That sounds like gold. But the cost-per-click in the Google Ads auction tells a different story: €1.78 and €1.89 respectively.

Compare that with Digitale Transformation Beratung (digital transformation consulting): just 720 monthly searches, but a CPC of €5.27. Advertisers are willing to pay nearly three times as much per click. That's the market telling us where the buyers actually are.

The high volumes rank for terms students, certification-seekers and job hunters search — not your buyers.

This fundamentally reorders the service lines. Digitalisierungsberatung (digitalization consulting) and Prozessoptimierung (process optimization) become the commercial anchors. Projektmanagement-Beratung (project management consulting) sits solidly in the middle. Agiles Projektmanagement and Requirements Engineering are deliberately positioned as content and authority plays — not conversion pages. Interim Product Owner remains a hub page with no SEO ambition; inquiries for that line come from LinkedIn and referrals, not Google.

What follows is the full data-driven case for this new order — and the architecture that operationalizes it.

Volume realism: City modifier keywords in Germany typically sit at 10–50 monthly searches per service-city combination. "Digitalisierungsberatung Köln" alone won't move mountains — but 40 such pages together generate 400–2,000 additional monthly searches.

More importantly: they rank surprisingly fast (KD typically < 15), because local SEO competition for consulting services in most German cities is unexpectedly thin.

Industry clusters per city: Where CX has strong existing relationships through Cologne Intelligence and prior cases (AOK Rheinland/Hamburg → statutory health insurance cluster in Köln and Hamburg; utility companies → Frankfurt/Düsseldorf), we tie industry use-case pages directly to the relevant city.

That stops the city pages from being generic "service in city" boilerplate and turns them into local credibility pieces — your real cases assembled into ranking material.

08 — Methodology

What's really behind the numbers.

Data source. Semrush API, database de, pulled on May 12, 2026 across three endpoints: phrase_this (volume, CPC, competition), phrase_kdi (Keyword Difficulty Index 0–100), and phrase_related (semantically related variants). All 12 head terms covered in full; 4 of 6 service clusters with full variant expansion. The two un-pulled clusters (Requirements Engineering, Interim Product Owner) are precisely the ones we're treating as content/authority and outbound assets respectively — so the gap doesn't change the strategy.

Commercial keyword filter. We keep only keywords with CPC ≥ €2.50 and monthly volume ≥ 50. The CPC filter is the hardest one: if advertisers aren't willing to pay at least €2.50 per click, the buyer share of search traffic is too low for a landing page to convert profitably.

Conversion funnel assumptions. Three levers: long-tail capture multiplier (3×), blended click-through rate at average position 3–5 (conservative 15%, stretch 22%), B2B consulting landing-page conversion rate (conservative 2.5%, stretch 4%). These values come from published benchmarks for DACH B2B consulting — intentionally set on the conservative side.

What the numbers don't include. Paid ads, LinkedIn outreach, referrals, direct traffic, inquiries gained through your existing network. The forecast is strictly the incremental lift from organic search via the 200-page architecture.

What the numbers might understate. Branded search (once CX is established as an authority), GEO/AEO inquiries from ChatGPT, Perplexity and Google AI Overviews, and inbound backlinks that further strengthen rankings — these secondary effects typically multiply inquiry numbers by 1.3–1.8× after month 6 in well-executed SEO builds.

Confidence level. The keyword volumes are real and replicable. The KD scores are too. The inquiry forecast is a model — we hold the floor variant (~100/mo at maturity) at roughly 80% probability of being achievable with disciplined execution.
